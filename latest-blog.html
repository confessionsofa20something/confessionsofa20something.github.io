I think of myself as a journaling pro since I’ve kept a pretty consistent journal since 2020. And let me say, its been a game-changer for my mental health. I tried to bottle every emotion up so as not to overwhelm or irritate my friends and family. But that did not work, and I had a quarter-life crisis every other week.

Writing all my thoughts and feelings in a physical journal felt so daunting, and the idea of someone accidentally stumbling across it made me shudder. If you feel the same, here’s a tip: The Apple notes app.

A very slept-on app, and its FREE! Who doesn’t love a freebie? A couple of key features of the notes app is the lock feature. You can put a passcode on each entry, which no one can access. That way, you don’t feel the need to censor your writing. It has come in clutch many times. Plus, if you’re an iPad kid like myself, and you prefer to write, not type your entries. You can do this with the apple pencil in the app; you can make doodles add photos, and spice up the page however you see fit. It’s amazing!

Another issue I had with journaling was where to start. I had so many thoughts rattling up in my mind, and it was hard to get pen to paper. A great way to overcome this is to identify why you want to journal. I started journaling to get to know myself better and understand who I was as a person. So, I started with a list of all my favourite random things, from Hamilton the musical to frozen yoghurt with pistachio spread. Think of it like the talking stage of a relationship but with yourself. Random, I know, but it got me started, and that’s all that matters.

My last tip for today is just to get started. Don’t overthink it! One year from today, you will be so glad you started so if you take anything from this. Just start writing.

Good luck x
